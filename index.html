Tamil Brahmin & Non-Brahmin Ceremonies

Tamil weddings (Kalyanam) are steeped in Vedic chants, ancient Tamil hymns, and joyful family engagement. Highlights include the **Kasi Yatra** where the groom playfully pretends to leave for Kasi to lead a life of asceticism, only to be stopped by the bride's father who offers his daughter's hand in marriage.

  • Oonjal (Swing Ceremony): The couple sits on an ornate wooden swing, sung traditional songs while family members feed them milk and bananas to ward off evil.
  • Kanyadaanam & Mangalyadharanam: The bride sits on her father's lap, and the sacred yellow thread (Thali) is tied around her neck with three knots.
  • Saptapadi: The couple takes seven steps around the sacred fire, sealing their commitment.

Telugu Vivaham Customs

Telugu weddings celebrate togetherness through colors, playfulness, and highly symbolic acts. The customs reflect the rich agricultural heritage and spiritual foundation of Andhra Pradesh and Telangana.

  • Jeelakarra Bellam: The bride and groom place a paste of cumin seeds (Jeelakarra) and jaggery (Bellam) on each other's heads. It symbolizes that they will stay united in sweet and bitter times.
  • Madhuparkam: The bride wears a red-bordered white saree, and the groom wears a white dhoti with a red border, representing purity and strength.
  • Talambralu: The couple showers each other with turmeric-colored pearls and rice in a fun, competitive ceremony.

Karnataka Marriage Rituals

Kannada weddings (Maduve) are known for their warm simplicity, deep focus on family respect, and joyous regional customs that connect the couple to their ancestors.

  • Nandi Puja: Performed prior to the wedding day to invoke peace and seek blessings from Lord Shiva and the bull Nandi.
  • Dharegrahana: Equivalent to Kanyadaanam, where the bride's father places her hand in the groom's, and coconut milk is poured over their hands.
  • Saptapadi: The bride walks seven steps, stepping on small stone heaps (representing hurdles in life) with the groom's guidance.

Malayali Wedding Ceremonies

Kerala weddings (Malyalee Vivaham) are typically brief, serene, and elegant. Usually held in temples or spacious halls in the morning, they focus on absolute purity and traditional white-and-gold aesthetic.

  • Kasavu Attire: The bride wears a traditional Kerala Kasavu saree, and the groom wears a gold-bordered mundu. Jasmine strings adorn the bride's hair.
  • Pudamuri: The groom gifts the bride a silk saree (Mundu-pudava) on a platter, symbolizing his promise to support her financially and emotionally.
  • Sadhya: A luxurious grand feast served on plantain leaves featuring up to 28-30 traditional vegetarian curries and payasams.
